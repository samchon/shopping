# ARTICLE Workshop

Critic panel:

- Mira: headline and hook
- Owen: backend credibility
- Jia: article rhythm and readability
- Pavel: technical precision
- Ren: virality and shareability

## Round 01

- Mira: Title is strong but too descriptive.
- Owen: Backend claim needs code evidence fast.
- Jia: TL;DR is fine, but early paragraphs ramble.
- Pavel: Use real controller and DTO comments.
- Ren: Need a cleaner "why this matters" line.
- Decision: keep the title for now, move code proof earlier.

## Round 02

- Mira: The opening quote works.
- Owen: "well-designed backend" is good; avoid "best backend."
- Jia: Paragraphs must stay short.
- Pavel: Do not over-explain SDK mechanics too early.
- Ren: Early emotional hook should target backend developers.
- Decision: keep tone humble, sharpen consequence.

- Mira: "If you are a backend developer..." is a good turn.

- Jia: Avoid too many one-line paragraphs in a row.

- Decision: keep the line, but tighten surrounding prose.

## Round 04

- Jia: Repetition is useful, but trim filler.
